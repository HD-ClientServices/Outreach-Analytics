The profile of those who close · from 66 won deals (closed won) · updates every 3 weeks, on Sundays
01 Firmographics
Profile: Owner-operator of a physical, cash-intensive business, 5 to 30 years old; healthy but drowning in debt, not bankrupt.
Industries: 55% trades/contractors, 15% transport/auto, 13% food/retail, 14% health/services. Almost zero e-commerce.
Debt: Median of $111k (range $20k to $520k); 9 of 10 are stacked in 2 to 6 MCAs.
Mindset: Thinks in dollars per week or per day, never in APR; naive about MCAs (“didn't know the rate was 56%”).
Geography: Spread across the US, weighted to the South and the Sun Belt; 1 in 6 is handled in Spanish.
Industry, geography and track record of the businesses that close
02 Pain points & context
The pain: A daily or weekly payment ($3k to $20k per week, some pay $5,000 a day) that strangles their cash flow. In their words: “money in the front door, out the back door”.
Stacking: 9 of 10 are stacked (“I borrow one to pay the other”). Current but drowning; the firm catches them before default.
Origin: They took the MCA defensively (to bridge a gap, a slow season, payroll), rarely for luxury.
Trigger: They answer the call when the pain gets acute: an unpayable debit, a UCC lien or a lawsuit, or they were already burned by a prior broker (1 in 3).
What hurts them and why they answer the call
03 Buying drivers
Relief now: The main driver. A payment they can afford; they close on hearing the new number (“that one I can pay without sweating”).
Legal protection: Stop the harassment, the lawsuits and the liens, with attorneys on their side.
Get unstuck: Escape the trap and unblock real credit (an SBA, a line).
Fairness: Pay what's fair, not the predatory rate or the inflated fees.
The key: They want to pay (“I'm not running from it”). The angle that closes is restructure, not erase: a payment 50 to 70% lower and attorneys who shield them.
What motivates them to do MCA debt settlement · dominant emotion: overwhelm + anger at being deceived
04 Typical objections & how to handle them
“Is this different from the one that already scammed me?” (the most common objection) → We're attorney-led from the start; we do this daily with their same lenders and cut the fees, charging nothing upfront.
“Will they keep harassing me or sue me?” → With power of attorney we take over all communication, the new agreement supersedes the original, and we handle the UCC/COJ.
“Do I have to pay upfront?” → No upfront (or just $500 to open escrow); the fee is already baked into the reduced payment.
“What about my credit? I don't want another MCA.” → It's not a loan or a consolidation, it's restructuring; MCAs don't report to credit.
“Let me think about it or talk to my partner.” → Anchor to the next debit: “we stop tomorrow's draft.” It's the weakest point of the close.

05 Communication style
Talks money like this: In dollars per week or per day, never in APR. Wants “one single monthly payment”.
Names their lenders: OnDeck, Forward, Rapid… Saying “we know those” builds instant trust.
Metrics they watch: The weekly or daily payment amount and the % reduction; not the term or the rate.
Their phrases: It's killing me · strangling my working capital · borrowing from Peter to pay Paul · I'm not running from it · I'm the one that got it, I wanna pay it.
